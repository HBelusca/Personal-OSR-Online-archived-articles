On May 2026, the OSR Online website went off. The maintainers claim:
> As of May 2026, we retired the contents of OSRONLINE entirely. It was great while it lasted. But all good things must come to an end.

How complicated is it to archive articles, at least in text-format, nowadays? It would appear this is very complicated, since no apparent efforts have been made by them to migrate the OSR Online contents to their new www.osr.com website... Seriously.

On a related note, accessing the automatically-archived articles via the Wayback Machine, turns out to not work properly, because of infinite self-redirections....

Thus, this repository aims to archive some of the articles I used personally, that I have been able to archive before their website went off.
